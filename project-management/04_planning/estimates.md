## 1. Introduction

### 1.1. Purpose
This document provides a detailed breakdown of the estimated effort, duration, and cost required to complete the "NetSuite Learning Path" project. These estimates are based on the work defined in the Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) and are used for scheduling, resource planning, and project tracking.

### 1.2. Estimation Methodology
The estimates in this document are derived using a combination of bottom-up estimation from the WBS and the **Three-Point Estimation (PERT)** technique. This approach helps to account for uncertainty and risk.

The following formula is used to calculate the expected effort (`E`):
*   **O** = Optimistic Estimate (best-case scenario)
*   **M** = Most Likely Estimate (realistic scenario)
*   **P** = Pessimistic Estimate (worst-case scenario)
*   **Formula:** `E = (O + 4M + P) / 6`

All effort estimates are provided in **person-hours (hrs)**.

## 6. Estimation Assumptions and Dependencies

*   **Assumption 1:** A NetSuite Sandbox / Development account is available at no cost for the duration of the project.
*   **Assumption 2:** The project will be executed by one person with an average availability of 20 hours per week.
*   **Assumption 3:** The individual has prior experience in programming (e.g., Python), databases (e.g., Oracle), and DevOps concepts, which is factored into the "refresher" and learning times.
*   **Assumption 4:** The scope of the portfolio projects will remain as defined in the project planning documents. Scope creep will invalidate these estimates.
*   **Dependency 1:** Progress is dependent on the availability and quality of online learning resources and documentation.

## 7. Risks and Contingency

A **20% contingency buffer** has been added to the total effort and duration estimates. This buffer is intended to cover risks such as:
*   **Risk 1:** Underestimation of the complexity of a specific topic (e.g., SuiteScript Map/Reduce scripts).
*   **Risk 2:** Technical difficulties with the sandbox environment or development tools.
*   **Risk 3:** Unplanned personal events that reduce the available hours per week.
*   **Risk 4:** The need for additional research to overcome a technical challenge.

The contingency buffer will be used as needed and its consumption will be tracked.
